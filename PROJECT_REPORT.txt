                    PROJECT REPORT: MR. NILA TEX WEBSITE

1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mr. Nila Tex is a premium clothing and textile showroom located in 
Addalaichenai, Eastern Province, Sri Lanka. The business has been operating 
since 2022 and specializes in providing quality clothing and textiles for the 
entire family.

The website serves as a digital storefront for this established business, 
representing their first significant step into digital marketing and online 
visibility. The primary purpose of this project was to create a modern, 
responsive e-commerce presence that showcases the business's products and 
services while bridging the gap between the physical showroom and potential 
customers who prefer to browse and research products online.

This website enables the business to reach a broader audience beyond their 
local customer base in Addalaichenai and surrounding areas, while providing 
convenient ways for customers to inquire about products and place orders.

2. OBJECTIVES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The website aims to achieve the following objectives:

Primary Objectives:
- Establish a strong online presence for Mr. Nila Tex
- Showcase the business's product collections for men, women, kids, and textiles
- Provide customers with easy access to business information and location
- Enable direct ordering through WhatsApp integration
- Promote special offers and seasonal sales campaigns
- Build customer trust through professional design and transparency

Target Audience:
- Local residents of Addalaichenai and surrounding areas (Kalmunai, Pottuvil)
- Customers seeking quality textiles and clothing in Sri Lanka
- Shoppers interested in traditional wear (sarees, shalwars, ethnic wear)
- Families looking for one-stop shopping for all clothing needs
- Both male and female customers aged 18-55
- Budget-conscious shoppers seeking quality within reasonable prices

3. PROJECT DESCRIPTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mr. Nila Tex website is a single-page business website designed specifically 
for a textile and clothing showroom. The website provides comprehensive 
information about the business and its offerings with smooth scroll navigation 
between sections.

Key Features and Sections:

Preloader: An animated loading screen displaying the brand name "NILA TEX" 
with stylish outline text effect that provides a professional first impression 
while the page loads. The preloader fades out smoothly after 1.5 seconds.

Navigation Bar: A fixed header containing the brand logo with gem icon, 
navigation menu links (Home, Collections, Categories, Offers, About, Contact), 
search functionality, and mobile hamburger menu. The navbar changes appearance 
on scroll (transparent to white background with shadow).

Hero Section: A full-screen hero slider showcasing three background images 
with overlay. Features animated brand name "NILA TEX" with gradient text effect, 
tagline "Premium Clothing & Textiles for the Whole Family", location display 
"Addalaichenai, Sri Lanka", and two call-to-action buttons ("Explore 
Collections" and "Visit Showroom"). Includes slider navigation dots at the 
bottom.

Features Bar: A horizontal bar displaying four key selling points with icons: 
Premium Quality (gem icon), Best Prices (tag icon), Personal Service (users 
icon), and Years of Service (store icon with "6+" dynamic counter).

Product Categories: Four distinct category cards with images featuring Men, 
Women, Kids, and Textiles/Fabrics sections. Each card has attractive hover 
effects with zoom, overlay gradient, and category tags.

Product Showcase: A dynamic product display grid that loads products externally 
from Google Sheets. Products show front and back images, category labels, 
product names, pricing with discount badges, and WhatsApp ordering buttons. 
Includes category filter buttons and real-time search functionality.

Special Offers: A promotional banner featuring Ramadan Special Sale with 
background image, overlay, pulsing badge animation, 30% discount announcement, 
and a live countdown timer showing days, hours, minutes, and seconds.

About Section: Company information with two showroom images, dynamic 
experience counter, company history, and key features list (Premium Quality, 
Personal Service, Best Prices) with icons.

Customer Testimonials: A carousel displaying three customer reviews with 
quote icons, 5-star ratings, customer photos, names, and locations. 
Includes navigation buttons and touch swipe support for mobile devices.

Contact Section: Features a Google Maps embed showing the showroom location 
(6VX3+6PW, Main St, Addalaichenai). Contact information includes phone 
number (075 455 2963), email (info@mrnilatex.lk), and operating hours 
(Mon-Sat 8:00 AM - 8:00 PM, Sunday 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM). Includes a contact 
form with name, phone, email, and message fields. Social media links for 
Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and TikTok.

Footer: Contains brand information, quick links, category links, newsletter 
signup form, social media connections, and dynamic copyright year.

Floating Elements:
- WhatsApp floating button (appears after scrolling past hero)
- Back to top button (appears after scrolling 500px)

4. TECHNOLOGIES USED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Frontend Technologies:

- HTML5: Semantic markup for page structure with proper document type
- CSS3: Modern styling with CSS variables, Grid, Flexbox, animations
- JavaScript (ES6+): Interactive functionality and dynamic content
- Font Awesome 6.5.1: Icon library for UI elements
- Google Fonts: Playfair Display (serif for headings) and Poppins (sans-serif for body)
- AOS Animation Library 2.3.1: Scroll-based animations
- CSS Custom Properties: Theme variables for colors and spacing

Backend/Data Management:

- Google Sheets: External data storage for products (CSV format)
- Google Forms: Product entry system for easy data management
- PapaParse 5.4.1: JavaScript library for parsing CSV data from Google Sheets
- LocalStorage: Client-side caching for better performance

External Services:

- Google Maps Embed API: Store location display
- Netlify: Hosting platform for the live website (mr-nilatex.netlify.app)
- WhatsApp API: Direct messaging functionality for orders

The website does not require a traditional backend as product data is managed 
through Google Sheets, making it easy for non-technical staff to update 
products without coding knowledge.

5. DESIGN & UI/UX
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Layout Structure:

The website follows a single-page design with smooth scroll navigation. 
Sections are arranged hierarchically in a logical order:
1. Preloader (loading screen)
2. Navigation (fixed header)
3. Hero (full-screen slider)
4. Features Bar (key selling points)
5. Categories (product categories)
6. Collections (dynamic product grid)
7. Offers (promotional banner)
8. About (company information)
9. Testimonials (customer reviews)
10. Contact (map and form)
11. Footer

This allows visitors to find information easily without navigating through 
multiple pages.

Color Scheme:

- Primary Black (#1a1a1a): Sophistication and elegance
- Gold (#c9a959): Luxury and premium quality association
- White (#ffffff): Clean and professional appearance
- Cream (#f8f6f3): Soft background tone
- Dark Gray (#4a4a4a): Body text color
- Medium Gray (#6a6a6a): Secondary text
- Soft Background (#f5f4f2): Section backgrounds

Typography:

- Headings: Playfair Display (serif font) - elegant and premium feel
  - Weights: 400, 500, 600, 700
  - Used for: Hero title, section titles, logo
- Body Text: Poppins (sans-serif font) - clean and modern readability
  - Weights: 300, 400, 500, 600, 700
  - Used for: Paragraphs, buttons, navigation, product info

Responsiveness:

The website is fully responsive with optimized layouts for:
- Desktop: 1024px and above (5-column product grid, 4-column features)
- Tablet: 768px to 1023px (reduced columns, adjusted spacing)
- Mobile: Below 768px (single column, stacked layouts)

Mobile Features:
- Hamburger menu for navigation
- Touch-friendly sliders and carousels
- Responsive grid layouts (2-3 columns)
- Larger tap targets for buttons (minimum 44px)
- Swipe-enabled testimonial carousel

9. CHALLENGES FACED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Technical Challenges:

Challenge 1: Loading Products from External Source
The product data is stored in Google Sheets which requires fetching and 
parsing CSV data. Solution implemented was using PapaParse library to 
convert CSV to JavaScript objects with error handling for failed requests. 
If Google Sheets is unavailable, the website displays "No products 
available at the moment" message instead of breaking.

Challenge 2: Google Drive Image Links
Product images stored in Google Drive cannot be displayed directly due to 
permission settings. Solution was creating a function that extracts the 
file ID from the Google Drive URL and generates a proper thumbnail URL 
in the format: https://drive.google.com/thumbnail?id={fileId}&sz=w800

Challenge 3: Mobile Menu Scroll Lock
When the mobile menu opens, the background page should not scroll. 
Solution was using fixed positioning with saved scroll position. When 
menu opens, the body position is set to fixed with top offset equal to 
negative scroll position. On close, the original scroll position is 
restored.

Challenge 4: Image Flip on Mobile Devices
Hover effects do not work on touch devices as there is no hover state. 
Solution was using CSS media query "@media (hover: none) and (pointer: 
coarse)" to disable flip animation on touch devices while keeping it 
for desktop users.

Challenge 5: Performance vs. Animation
Balancing visual appeal with page load speed was challenging. Solution 
was using AOS (Animate On Scroll) library efficiently with once:true 
option, so animations only play once when elements come into view.

Challenge 6: Dynamic Content Generation
The website needed to calculate years of experience and copyright year 
automatically. Solution was using JavaScript to get the current year 
and calculate experience based on CONFIG.SHOP_START_YEAR (2022).

Design Challenges:

Challenge 7: Color-Coded Discount Badges
Different discount percentages needed different colors for visual 
distinction. Solution was implementing CONFIG.DISCOUNT_THRESHOLDS 
with color mappings (SUPER: 50%+, HIGH: 30%+, MEDIUM: 15%+).

Challenge 8: Product Badge Colors
Product badges needed to be color-coded based on keywords (New, Hot, 
Sale, Trend, Limited, Best Seller) or category. Solution was creating 
getBadgeStyle() function with keyword detection and fallback to 
category colors.

Challenge 9: Search UX on Mobile
Full-screen search overlay blocked product viewing. Solution was 
creating a dropdown-style overlay that doesn't block the page, 
allowing users to see products while searching.

Challenge 10: Google Form Integration
Need for easy product management without spreadsheet editing. Solution 
was creating Google Form linked to sheet with auto-timestamp for 
automatic sorting of new products.

11. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Mr. Nila Tex website successfully achieves its objective of creating a 
professional online presence for the textile business. The single-page 
design provides an excellent user experience while maintaining simplicity 
for both visitors and content managers. The website effectively showcases 
the business's products and services while providing multiple channels for 
customer engagement.

The integration of Google Forms for product entry and WhatsApp ordering 
provides a convenient way for customers to make purchases without complex 
e-commerce infrastructure, making it ideal for small businesses in Sri Lanka 
where WhatsApp is widely used for communication. The Google Sheets-based 
product management system allows non-technical staff to easily update products 
without coding knowledge.

The recent enhancements including caching, data validation, and improved 
search functionality have made the website more robust and user-friendly. 
The timestamp-based sorting from Google Forms ensures new products always 
appear first, keeping the inventory fresh and encouraging repeat visits.

Key Learning Outcomes:
- Understanding of responsive web design principles
- Integration of external data sources (Google Sheets, Google Forms)
- Implementation of WhatsApp business integration
- Client-side caching strategies
- Data validation and error handling
- SEO best practices for local businesses
- Mobile-first design approach
- Performance optimization techniques
- CSS animations and transitions
- JavaScript DOM manipulation and event handling
- SVG icon implementation
- Accessibility considerations in web design

This project demonstrates that with modern web technologies, small businesses 
can establish a professional online presence without significant investment 
in complex e-commerce systems. The combination of Google Sheets/Forms for 
product management and WhatsApp for order processing provides a practical 
and maintainable solution that is well-suited to the Sri Lankan market.
